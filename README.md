# HUMIS

## GDES 2343: Interaction Design.

This initial project taught me the typical process the development of a webpage. The steps of 
starting with a wireframe of the general structure, inserting information, styling, and then overall edits
is something I am using in my other webdesign classes. This process keeps my code organized and helps avoid 
unnecessary restructuring after fully styling a section of the page. In my future designs I 
can use these lessons to avoid wasting time, a valuable resource in the life of a busy person.

Learning how to view a design in different sections has changed the way I look at websites. When translating
designs this assignment taught me how to look at elements like the nav and see how they can share snippets of
code. Seeing how parts of the design fit inside each other is important in a markup language where hierarchy is
very important. This critical eye I am starting to develop allows for me to implement a design efficiently.

Overall this assignment taught me how to identify areas of a design based on similarities and sections that 
logically work with the structure of HTML. I now have the basic knowledge base to create helpful wireframes
to avoid wasting time when translating my designs into HTML and CSS. I have an understanding of how much work
it takes to translate a design into a webpage and can use this knowledge to influence how I approach webdesign
in the future.
